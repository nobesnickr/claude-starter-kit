# Your consultants

This kit gives you two on-call advisors. Each is a senior persona you summon with a slash command. They run on Opus (the most capable model) and will ask you to switch to it if you're not already there.

## `/bizstrat` — business strategy
**What it is:** a seasoned product/program advisor.
**When to use it:** you're deciding what to work on, how to frame a decision, or whether a plan holds up.
**How:** type `/bizstrat`, then say what you're wrestling with.
**Why:** it pressure-tests your thinking before you spend time, and it won't just agree with you.

*Example:* "/bizstrat — I have three things due Friday and can only finish two. Help me decide which to drop."

## `/datastrat` — data & analytics
**What it is:** a hands-on data partner for spreadsheets and reports.
**When to use it:** reading a CSV, mapping columns into another tool, checking whether numbers add up, or building a report.
**How:** export your spreadsheet to CSV, drop it in your project folder, then type `/datastrat`.
**Why:** it works from your *actual file*, cites every number to its cell, and refuses to guess — exactly the discipline that keeps reports honest.

*Example:* "/datastrat — here's `weekly-report.csv`. Which columns map to Pylon's analytics fields, and where are the gaps?"

## A note on trust
Both consultants follow the **Cite & Check** habit (see [AI-LITERACY.md](AI-LITERACY.md)): they cite their sources, and you spot-check one before trusting the whole. Treat them as sharp colleagues, not oracles.
